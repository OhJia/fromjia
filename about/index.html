Hi there.

My name is Jia, pronounciation: ja. Or if you'd like a challenge Jiashan Wu, pronounciation: jiāshān wú



I am a designer, technologist, and researcher. I'm a graduate student in the NYU Interactive Telecommunications Program and Partner at Dorm Room Fund. Currently, I'm working on p5 Mobile as part of Google Summer of Code.



Before diving into the "Center for the Recently Possible", I worked with early stage startups, enterprises, and nonprofits as a designer and art director. I co-founded and managed XOlator, a creative interactive agency that designs and build web platforms, applications, and data visualizations. I researched the design and manufacturing of Shanzhai phones in Shenzhen, China as a Fulbright scholar. I graduated from Parsons School of Design majoring in Communication Design.



These days I'm excited by the capabilities of mobile and connected devices to create new experiences. I'm getting better at front end development with HTML/CSS/JavaScript, server side programming with Node.js, mobile development with Cordova/JavaScript and Swift, and making wireless connections with BLE and cheap radios (it's like magic!).



On a clear day, you can find me biking in the friendly New York traffic and in Brooklyn, where I reside.
