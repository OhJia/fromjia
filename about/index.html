Hi there.

I'm Jiashan Wu. Everyone calls me Jia, pronounciation: ja.



I'm a designer and coder based in Brooklyn, NY. I explore ways to create experiences for people. Last year I enrolled as a graduate student in NYU Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP), where I'm learning and playing in classes with names like Always On Always Connected, Networked Sensors for Development, and Towers of Power. I'm a Partner and Community Manager at Dorm Room Fund, a student-run venture firm backed by First Round Capital. I'm working on an open source project, p5 Mobile, as part of Google Summer of Code.



Before ITP, I co-founded and managed XOlator, an interactive agency that designs and build web platforms, applications, and data visualizations. We worked with clients like Interior Design Magazine, Nike, and What's Trending. My design journey started when I was 9, drawing in Microsoft paint and printing them on stickers which I sold to my classmates to buy pretzels. Then I went to Parsons School of Design and majored in Communication Design. Once upon a time, I also shadowed makers and distributors of Shanzhai phones in Shenzhen, China for a year as a Fulbright scholar.



I'm building technical skills in mobile, front-end, back-end development, and connected devices. Some of the tools and languages I'm learning and using include JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Node.js, Cordova, Swift, Arduino, BLE sensors. Photoshop and Illustrator are my old friends, though I also like to use new protyping tools as well as project management tools.



I've worked with many talented people and am happy to be on a team. Feel free to say hi!
